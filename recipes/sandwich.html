Description

Get ready to chuckle and challenge your culinary fortitude with this Exasperatingly Complicated "Simple" Sandwich. It's the kind of recipe that makes you question whether you're a master chef or an unwitting participant in a cooking comedy show. With more steps than you can count and a final result that's anything but "simple," this sandwich will have you laughing, sighing, and maybe even shedding a tear of frustration. But hey, they say laughter is the best seasoning!

Ingredients

Bread

  • 2 ⅛ teaspoons active breath yeast
  • 1 ½ cups lukewarm glacier water (imported from the most remote peaks)
  • 3 ⅔ cups heirloom, stone-ground whole wheat flour A dash of unicorn tears (for that mythical umami)

Spread

  • 5 teaspoons fermented rainbow sauerkraut
  • 2 tablespoons hand-whisked, free-range dill mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon mischievously minced capers
  • Zest of a lemon that has seen a sunrise on Mars

For the "Impossibly Simple" Lettuce

  • 3 leaves of organically grown, hand-harvested microgreens
  • Spritz of dewdrops collected on the first day of spring

Stack

  • 6 slices of artisanal heirloom tomato (each with its own backstory)
  • 4 paper-thin slices of melancholic red onion 8 pickles that underwent a rigorous existential crisis

Steps

  1. Begin a journey of self-discovery by gazing into the void of your pantry
  2. Find the courage to locate yeast, then dissolve it in lukewarm glacier water that secretly yearns for tropical beaches
  3. Introduce heirloom flour, one teaspoon at a time, to the water mixture while whispering words of encouragement to each granule
  4. Knead the dough as if it's the meaning of life, then cover it and let it rise in a warm, nurturing corner of your kitchen where existential doubts are minimal
  5. Return later to a dough that has probably transcended earthly existence
  6. Punch it down, giving it a taste of the human condition. Bake the bread in an oven preheated to an arbitrarily specific temperature, somewhere between "slightly warm" and "vaguely toasty."
  7. Blend the sauerkraut, mayonnaise, capers, and lemon zest while reflecting on the complexities of flavor and life's fleeting nature
  8. Layer the microgreens with dewdrop spritzes, contemplating the absurdity of existence in a single leaf
  9. Assemble the sandwich with the bread, spread, and stack, paying tribute to the struggles of sandwich artists past
